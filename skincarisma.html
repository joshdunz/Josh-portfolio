Project summary

SkinCarisma Website Redesign

Redesign a webpage for a cosmetic product recommender, making it responsive and more user-friendly

As a personal project, I wanted to redesign a webpage that I used frequently. I chose this because I have greatly benefitted from the tool as someone who has sensitive skin and is very particular about cosmetic chemcials. While I find it useful, it's interface is not user friendly making it difficult for others to use.

Problem statement

Problem

SkinCarisma is not user-friendly

Objective

Make SkinCarisma more easy to use. And practice making responsive websites.

Info

Tools

HTML • CSS

Time

1 Week

Tags

Responsive Redesign • Accesibility • User-interviews

Design-process

01 Conduct User Interviews

I conducted usability test with potential users and recorded points where they faced difficulties. I also clarified issues along the way and recorded them down as pain points

02 Sketching

Based on these issue, I sketched out quick ideas I had on a piece of paper. Based on the "crazy-8" excersise, I gave myself 1 minute per sketch to come up with ideas for a total of 8 sketches. I then narrowed down the best ideas to one final sketch

03 Lofi mockups for each screen size

In Figma created black and white mockups focussing on page organization. I also modeled how the page would look at different sizes (Desktop, Tablet, Mobile) to make the page responsive

04 Hifi Prototype

I then mocked up exactly what the interface would look like in Figma for each of the screen sizes

05 Coding

With the Hifi Prototype as a reference, I coded the website, implementing buttons and interactive elements

Problems with the existing interface

User Research

My research through usability test found that users found it difficult to identify what the website could do. I also found them squinting at the text. They cited the difficulty reading and understanding headers as an issue. Most pointed to the fact that the images were not loaded on the page

Brainstorm ideas

Sketches

I sketched out 9 interfaces in quick succession just to get ideas flowing. Then I picked the best features and created a final sketch

Early Prototypes

Lofi mockups and responsive screens

I designed 3 screens based on the sketches and picked a color scheme to ensure a cohesive brand image

Final Prototypes

Hifi Mockups and building a style guide

I designed a style guide inline with the blue theme that focused on providing contrast, and readability. I also chose a color scheme that was soothing and conveyed trust-worthiness, based the purpose of the website.
