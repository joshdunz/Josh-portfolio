Project summary

Uno Health

Worked with a Medicare benefit startup to make their interface more accessible for the elderly and their caregivers

For a class project, my group was matched with Uno Health - a startup that aims to increase accesibility to Medicare. They do so by making it easier to apply for Medicare Savings online through their portal. While looking at ways to expand, they realised that leveraging caregivers who were more digitally savy was a key way to lower the barrier-to-entry for this huge market. That's where our team came in.

Problem statement

Problem

Many Medicare eligible members have caregivers or family members who manage their health and financial documents. Uno Health's portal currently has little functionality to enable caregivers to be involved in the medicare enrolment process.

Objective

  1. Design a portal interface and flow which enables a caregiver to initiate the Medicare enrolment process on behalf of the individual they are caring for - (Caree)
  2. Enable a Caree to invite their caregiver to support their application process on the portal

Design-process

01 Clarify brief & Identify pain points

My team and I read and annoted the brief with clarifying questions in the format "[This is what I interpreted], is this what you mean or can you clarify". We paid particular attention to identifying specific pain points and the ask of the client.

02 Rough Sketches

Each of us drew rough sketches of ways to solve the problems identified. We then brought our sketches together to brainstorm ideas for an interface. We worked through differences to arrive at a flow combining our best ideas.

03 Lo-Fi Mockups & Feedback

We worked together on a shared figma document on a black and white mockup, focusing on key features and page organization, disgarding aesthetics. I worked on the documents upload screens because I had a clear idea for making the process more organized. We also checked-in with our professors and implemented their suggestions.

04 High-Fi Mockups

I was in charge of designing the style guide which was used by my team to ensure a cohesive aesthetic. In my document screens, I focused on using colors green and red to show success and alerts respectively. I also worked with a teammate to implement a solution to the problem of users not knowing what documents to upload.

05 Check-in with client

We then shared the High Fidelity Mockups with our client through our Figma document and a loom video walking through the interface ahead of our schedule live meeting so our client had time to organize her feedback. During our live meeting I took notes for revisions and summarized and clarified the feedback before ending the call

06 Revisions

The feedback was focussed on using more readable fonts for visually challenged users and creating a "happy path" through button colors to nudge users in an ideal direction. We implemented the changes and submitted our finalized project to our professors and the client, concluding the project

Solution overview

We created a new flow to accomodate caregivers

We added new flow which enabled caregivers to create an account on behalf of their caree or support a caree who has their own existing account. We also implemented an authorization step to reduce scams and ways for caregivers who aren't physically present to request for documents stored with their caree.

Feedback & Iteration

Feedback from Professors & Clients from initial wireframes

The interface is not very accessible

Our critique from our classmates indicated that the font and login button was too small. They also advised us to include accessibilty features like a font scroll bar

There is no personalization

Our professor gave us the idea of adding the individual's or caree's name to the screen text to make the experience more human and personal.

There is no way for caregivers/carees to help if one does not have the app.

Our client suggested that there should be an off-app upload option for carees who aren't very tech savy to send their caregivers the requested documents.

Reflections

Reflections

Client work

I learn't that it is important to first have a clear idea of what a client wants so it can be clarified later. This format of hypothesis testing was an approach I tried and was proven effective

Group Work

This was my first time working with a group on an external client project. I found that the best ways to resolve disagreements is explaining though processes for how this addresses user pain points. I also learned that there are many equally good solutions to a problem. I really enjoyed the team experience.

Creating a happy path

I from client feedback learned the concept of a happy path - an ideal path through which a user would have the best experience. I had the opportunity to put into practice using visual nudges to prompt users to follow it and I will apply this concept to future projects.
